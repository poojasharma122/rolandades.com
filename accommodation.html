Types of Accommodation

Luxury Lodges and Hotels

For travellers looking to indulge, New Zealand offers world-class lodges and hotels set in breathtaking locations. Enjoy premium service, fine dining, and stunning views of mountains, lakes, or coastline. Popular areas for luxury stays include Queenstown, Wanaka, and the Bay of Islands.

Boutique Hotels and Bed & Breakfasts

If you prefer a more personal touch, boutique hotels and B&Bs provide cosy atmospheres with local charm. Many are family-run and offer insight into local culture and cuisine. These options are found in towns like Rotorua, Wellington, and Christchurch.

Motels and Holiday Parks

Motels offer convenient, affordable accommodation close to towns and transport routes. Holiday parks provide camping, powered sites for campervans, and cabins, making them ideal for families and budget travellers exploring the country by road.

Backpackers and Hostels

Perfect for solo travellers and those on a budget, hostels in New Zealand are known for friendly atmospheres and community spaces. They often organize group activities and provide kitchen facilities. Popular hostel towns include Auckland, Christchurch, and Queenstown.

Farmstays and Unique Stays

For a unique experience, stay on a working farm or in eco-lodges, treehouses, and boutique retreats. These offer authentic connections with nature and rural life. Regions like the Coromandel Peninsula and Marlborough have several farmstay options.

🗺️Where to Stay by Region

Auckland: Wide range from city hotels to waterfront apartments and backpacker hostels.

Rotorua: Thermal resorts, motels, and Māori cultural lodges.

Wellington: Central city hotels, boutique B&Bs, and budget hostels.

Queenstown & Wanaka: Luxury lodges, lakeside hotels, motels, and holiday parks.

Fiordland: Limited lodges and camping sites; book early for Milford Sound area.

Bay of Islands: Beachfront resorts, holiday parks, and quaint inns.

💡Booking Tips

Peak travel seasons (summer and school holidays) fill up quickly — book well in advance.

Use reputable booking platforms and cross-check reviews.

Consider staying in smaller towns near major tourist spots for better rates and authentic experiences.

Check cancellation policies carefully, especially during variable weather seasons.

🛎️Accessibility & Amenities

Many hotels and lodges offer accessible rooms and facilities.

If you require special assistance or features like Wi-Fi, parking, or breakfast included, check directly with accommodation providers.
