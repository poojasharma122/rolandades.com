Things To Do

Things to Do in New Zealand

New Zealand offers an incredible variety of activities for all interests and energy levels. From adrenaline-pumping adventures and scenic hikes to cultural experiences and peaceful nature escapes, there’s something to suit every traveller. Use this guide to discover the top things to do during your trip.

Outdoor Adventures

Hiking New Zealand’s Great Walks

Experience some of the world’s finest trails on New Zealand’s renowned Great Walks. These multi-day treks traverse stunning landscapes including alpine passes, ancient forests, and rugged coastlines.

  • Milford Track (Fiordland National Park) — Often called “the finest walk in the world,” this 53.5 km trail leads through fjords, waterfalls, and beech forests.

  • Tongariro Alpine Crossing (North Island) — A challenging day hike across volcanic terrain featuring emerald lakes and steaming vents.

  • Routeburn Track (Fiordland/Mount Aspiring) — A spectacular 32 km hike linking Fiordland and Mount Aspiring National Parks.

  • Abel Tasman Coast Track (South Island) — Famous for golden beaches, native bush, and crystal-clear waters.

Water-Based Activities

New Zealand’s extensive coastline and pristine lakes make it perfect for water lovers.

  • Kayaking & Paddleboarding — Explore sheltered bays, fjords, and lake shores. Abel Tasman National Park and Milford Sound are top spots.

  • Jet Boating — Feel the rush on the Shotover and Kawarau Rivers near Queenstown.

  • Whale & Dolphin Watching — Kaikōura is a world leader for marine wildlife tours.

  • Surfing — Catch waves on famous west coast beaches like Raglan and Piha.

Cultural Experiences

Explore Māori Culture

Gain deeper understanding and appreciation of Aotearoa’s Indigenous heritage by visiting marae (meeting grounds), attending traditional performances, and learning about Māori history and customs.

Rotorua —The heartland of Māori culture, offering cultural villages, hangi feasts, and kapa haka shows.

Scenic Drives and Nature Spots

Drive Iconic Routes

Take road trips through diverse landscapes and quaint towns.

Milford Road — Winds through Fiordland to Milford Sound with spectacular views.

Pacific Coast Highway — Runs from Auckland to the Bay of Islands along rugged coastline

The Thermal Explorer Highway — Connects Rotorua, Taupō, and Napier through geothermal and volcanic landscapes.

Visit National Parks

Aside from the Great Walks, New Zealand’s national parks offer day hikes, wildlife viewing, and peaceful natural surroundings.

Abel Tasman National Park — Beaches, forests, and kayaking.

Tongariro National Park — Volcanic landscapes and alpine lakes.

Mount Cook National Park — Glaciers and mountain peaks.

Extreme & Adventure Sports

For adrenaline junkies, New Zealand is a playground.

Bungee Jumping —Sites include Kawarau Bridge (world’s first commercial bungee) and Nevis Bungy in Queenstown.

Skydiving —Tandem jumps with breathtaking views over Queenstown, Wanaka, and Auckland.

Canyoning & White-Water Rafting —Adventure through river gorges and rapids around Rotorua and Queenstown.

Paragliding & Hang GlidingGlide over landscapes for an unforgettable perspective.

Food & Wine Experiences

Taste New Zealand

From fresh seafood to world-class wines, food lovers will find plenty to enjoy.

Wine Regions — Visit Central Otago, Marlborough, and Hawke’s Bay for vineyard tours and tastings.

Seafood — Sample green-lipped mussels, Bluff oysters, and freshly caught fish.

Farmers Markets — Explore local produce and artisanal foods in cities and towns across the country.

Food Festivals — Keep an eye out for events like the Hokitika Wild Food Festival or the Wellington On a Plate culinary festival.

🛍️

Shopping & Local Markets

Queenstown and Wanaka — Boutiques, galleries, and artisan shops.

Auckland’s Ponsonby and Britomart — Trendy areas for fashion and design

Rotorua Māori Arts & Crafts — Find authentic carvings, jewellery, and textiles.

🛏️

Planning Your Activities

    Book popular activities like bungy jumping, skydiving, and guided hikes well in advance, especially during peak seasons.

    Check weather conditions regularly — mountain and coastal weather can change quickly.

    Respect local customs and natural environments to help preserve New Zealand’s beauty for future travellers.
