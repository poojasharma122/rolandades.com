Hidden Gems: Off-the-Beaten-Path Places to Explore

Introduction

While New Zealand’s iconic destinations attract global attention, some of the most rewarding travel experiences can be found in the quieter corners of the country. These hidden gems offer unique landscapes, cultural richness, and a deeper connection to Aotearoa’s natural heritage — all without the crowds.

Whanganui River Journey

Instead of walking, paddle your way through history. The Whanganui River Journey is one of New Zealand’s Great Walks — done by canoe. Over several days, you'll travel through dense forest, Māori heritage sites, and peaceful waterscapes.

The Forgotten World Highway

Winding between Taumarunui and Stratford, this scenic route passes through remote countryside, tunnels carved by hand, and the self-proclaimed “Republic of Whangamōmona.” It’s a drive rich in local history and rugged charm.

Mōrere Hot Springs

Tucked away in Hawke’s Bay, Mōrere Springs offers a tranquil soak in mineral-rich waters surrounded by rainforest. A local favourite, it’s a quiet alternative to the more commercial hot spring destinations.

Okarito

This West Coast settlement is a haven for birdlife and nature lovers. Kayak across New Zealand’s largest unmodified wetland or hike up to the Trig Viewpoint for coastal and mountain panoramas.

Pūtangirua Pinnacles

These otherworldly rock formations in the Wairarapa were featured in The Lord of the Rings. A short hike through forest and riverbed leads to this eerie, dramatic landscape — perfect for photographers.

Conclusion

These lesser-known spots offer a chance to slow down and experience the country’s rich tapestry in a more personal way. For those looking to go beyond the usual, New Zealand’s hidden gems provide discovery, solitude, and unforgettable stories.
